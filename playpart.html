The Master Builder Act 1 - Henrik Ibsen

SOLNESS.



In that case, I should simply have had to go racking my brains to discover what the knot could mean.



HILDA.



Oh yes, I suppose there are trolls of that kind in the world, too.



SOLNESS.



[Rises slowly.]

What a good thing it is that you have come to me now.



HILDA.



[Looks deeply into his eyes.]

Is it a good thing!



SOLNESS.



For I have been so lonely here. I have been gazing so helplessly at it all.

[In a lower voice.]

I must tell you--I have begun to be afraid of the younger generation.



HILDA.



[With a little snort of contempt.]

Pooh--is the younger generation something to be afraid of?



SOLNESS.



It is indeed. And that is why I have locked and barred myself in.

[Mysteriously.]

I tell you the younger generation will one day come and thunder at my door! They will break in upon me!
